The 56 Minor Arcana Cards

The Four Suits

The Minor Arcana comprises 56 cards across four suits, each connected to an element and domain of human experience.

Understanding the Minor Arcana

While the Major Arcana deals with life's great themes and spiritual lessons, the Minor Arcana addresses the everyday experiences, emotions, challenges, and triumphs that fill our daily lives. These 56 cards divide into four suits of 14 cards each: numbered cards from Ace through Ten, plus four Court Cards (Page, Knight, Queen, and King).

Each suit corresponds to one of the four classical elements, governing a specific realm of human experience. Understanding these elemental associations provides the foundation for interpreting any Minor Arcana card you encounter.

Suit of Cups - Water and Emotions

💧 Water Element

Domain: Emotions, Relationships, Love, Creativity, Intuition

The Suit of Cups governs the realm of feelings, relationships, emotional fulfillment, and creative expression. Like water itself, Cup cards flow with the currents of the heart: love, compassion, joy, grief, and the full spectrum of human emotion. When Cups dominate a reading, the situation is primarily emotional in nature, involving matters of the heart, creative projects, or intuitive experiences.

Cups cards often appear when relationships are the central concern, whether romantic partnerships, friendships, family bonds, or your relationship with yourself. They also govern artistic expression, dreams, and the inner world of imagination and feeling.

When abundant in a reading: Emotions are running high; relationships are at the forefront; trust your feelings. When absent: You may be disconnected from your emotions; consider what you are avoiding feeling.

Number Progression in Cups

  • Ace: New emotional beginning, overflowing love, spiritual connection
  • Two: Partnership, mutual attraction, harmonious connection
  • Three: Celebration, friendship, joyful community
  • Four: Contemplation, apathy, re-evaluation of what matters
  • Five: Loss, grief, regret, but also remaining blessings
  • Six: Nostalgia, childhood memories, innocence, generosity
  • Seven: Fantasy, illusion, choices, wishful thinking
  • Eight: Walking away, seeking deeper meaning, emotional courage
  • Nine: Contentment, wish fulfillment, emotional satisfaction
  • Ten: Lasting happiness, family harmony, emotional completion

Suit of Pentacles - Earth and Material World

🌱 Earth Element

Domain: Finances, Career, Health, Material Security, Physical World

The Suit of Pentacles (also called Coins or Disks) represents the material dimension of existence: money, work, physical health, home, and tangible resources. Earth energy is practical, patient, and focused on building lasting foundations. When Pentacles appear in a reading, the situation involves real-world concerns like career decisions, financial matters, property, or physical wellbeing.

Pentacles cards remind us that the spiritual and material are not separate; how we handle our resources, maintain our bodies, and build our earthly lives is itself a spiritual practice. These cards value craftsmanship, patience, and the steady accumulation of wisdom through hands-on experience.

When abundant in a reading: Material concerns need attention; focus on practical matters; invest in long-term stability. When absent: Material concerns may not be the priority; or you may be neglecting practical matters.

Number Progression in Pentacles

  • Ace: New financial opportunity, material blessing, fresh start
  • Two: Balance, adaptability, juggling priorities
  • Three: Teamwork, skilled craftsmanship, collaboration
  • Four: Security, stability, but also possessiveness
  • Five: Hardship, financial loss, feeling left out in the cold
  • Six: Generosity, giving and receiving, balance of resources
  • Seven: Patience, assessment, waiting for results to grow
  • Eight: Diligence, mastery, dedicated practice and skill-building
  • Nine: Abundance, luxury, self-sufficiency, enjoying results
  • Ten: Legacy, wealth, family prosperity, lasting success

Suit of Swords - Air and the Mind

💨 Air Element

Domain: Thoughts, Communication, Conflict, Truth, Intellect

The Suit of Swords represents the mental realm: thoughts, beliefs, communication, truth, and the double-edged nature of the intellect. Like air itself, Swords energy is swift, sharp, and can be both clarifying and cutting. These cards often appear in situations involving decisions, conflicts, honest communication, or the need to cut through confusion with the blade of truth.

Swords cards can feel challenging because they often deal with difficult truths, mental struggles, and necessary confrontations. However, they also represent clarity, justice, intellectual breakthroughs, and the liberation that comes from seeing things as they truly are rather than as we wish them to be.

When abundant in a reading: Mental activity is intense; communication matters; truth needs to be spoken or heard. When absent: The situation may be more emotional or practical than intellectual.

Number Progression in Swords

  • Ace: Mental clarity, breakthrough, new perspective, truth revealed
  • Two: Difficult decision, denial, stalemate, needing to choose
  • Three: Heartbreak, painful truth, sorrow, grief from revelation
  • Four: Rest, recuperation, mental retreat, meditation
  • Five: Conflict, defeat, hollow victory, choosing battles
  • Six: Transition, moving forward, leaving difficulty behind
  • Seven: Deception, strategy, stealth, thinking outside the box
  • Eight: Feeling trapped, self-imposed limitations, mental prison
  • Nine: Anxiety, nightmares, worry, mental anguish
  • Ten: Rock bottom, endings, the worst is over, dawn approaches

Suit of Wands - Fire and Spirit

🔥 Fire Element

Domain: Passion, Ambition, Energy, Creativity, Spiritual Drive

The Suit of Wands channels the element of fire: passion, ambition, creative energy, and spiritual drive. These cards burn with enthusiasm, confidence, and the urge to act. When Wands appear in a reading, they speak to your passions, career ambitions, creative projects, and the vital force that propels you forward in life. They are cards of action, adventure, and bold initiative.

Wands energy is inspiring but can also be challenging: fire that burns too hot leads to burnout, aggression, or scattered energy. The progression through the Wands tells the story of how we ignite our passions, sustain them through challenges, and ultimately master the creative fire within us.

When abundant in a reading: Energy is high; passion drives the situation; take bold action; creative projects are favored. When absent: Energy may be low; motivation needs rekindling; consider what excites you.

Number Progression in Wands

  • Ace: Inspiration, new passion, creative spark, bold beginning
  • Two: Planning, future vision, deciding direction, personal power
  • Three: Expansion, foresight, progress, looking to the horizon
  • Four: Celebration, homecoming, harmony, milestone achieved
  • Five: Competition, conflict, diverse opinions, creative tension
  • Six: Victory, recognition, public success, confidence
  • Seven: Perseverance, standing your ground, defending position
  • Eight: Swift action, momentum, rapid progress, messages
  • Nine: Resilience, persistence, last stretch before completion
  • Ten: Burden, responsibility, hard work, nearing the finish line

Understanding Court Cards

Each suit contains four Court Cards that represent personality types, aspects of yourself, or people in your life. They progress in maturity and mastery from Page through King:

Pages - The Students

Pages represent youthful energy, curiosity, and the beginning stages of exploring their suit's element. They bring messages, new opportunities for learning, and fresh perspectives. In a reading, a Page can indicate a child or young person, a new venture in its early stages, or the need to adopt a beginner's mindset.

  • Page of Cups - Emotional openness, creative messages, intuitive child
  • Page of Pentacles - Studious, practical opportunity, new skill
  • Page of Swords - Curious mind, new ideas, vigilant communication
  • Page of Wands - Enthusiastic explorer, creative potential, adventure calls

Knights - The Seekers

Knights are in active pursuit of their element's qualities. They move with purpose, sometimes recklessly, driven by the energy of their suit. Knights indicate action, movement, and change. They can represent young adults, the active pursuit of goals, or situations in rapid motion.

  • Knight of Cups - Romantic idealist, following the heart, creative pursuit
  • Knight of Pentacles - Methodical worker, reliable, steady progress
  • Knight of Swords - Swift thinker, direct communicator, charging ahead
  • Knight of Wands - Adventurer, passionate action, confident risk-taker

Queens - The Nurturers

Queens embody their element's inward expression with mastery and grace. They have internalized their suit's wisdom and express it through nurturing, creating, and holding space. Queens represent mature feminine energy (regardless of gender), emotional intelligence, and the power of receptive mastery.

  • Queen of Cups - Empathic healer, emotional depth, intuitive mastery
  • Queen of Pentacles - Practical nurturer, abundance creator, grounded wisdom
  • Queen of Swords - Clear communicator, independent thinker, honest truth-teller
  • Queen of Wands - Charismatic leader, confident creator, warm inspiration

Kings - The Masters

Kings represent the fullest outward expression and mastery of their element. They lead, direct, and shape the world through their suit's energy. Kings indicate authority, expertise, and mature masculine energy (regardless of gender). They have achieved command over their domain and can guide others with wisdom.

  • King of Cups - Emotional wisdom, calm authority, compassionate leader
  • King of Pentacles - Financial mastery, generous provider, stable success
  • King of Swords - Intellectual authority, fair judgment, clear decision-maker
  • King of Wands - Visionary leader, bold entrepreneur, inspiring commander
