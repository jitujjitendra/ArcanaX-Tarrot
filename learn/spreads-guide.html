Reading Techniques

Tarot Spreads Guide

Learn how to lay out and interpret tarot cards using popular spread patterns. Each spread offers a different lens for understanding your question.

What Is a Tarot Spread?

A tarot spread is a predefined pattern for laying out cards, where each position carries a specific meaning. The spread provides structure and context to your reading, helping you interpret cards not in isolation but in relation to each other and to specific aspects of your question. Choosing the right spread for your question is an important skill that develops with practice.

Think of a spread as a lens: the same cards might appear regardless of which spread you use, but the spread determines how you read them. A card in the "challenge" position tells a very different story than the same card in the "outcome" position.

Single Card Pull

Best For: Daily guidance, simple questions, building card familiarity

The simplest and most versatile spread. Draw one card as a focus for your day, a quick answer to a question, or a meditation prompt. Despite its simplicity, a single card can provide profound insight when approached with genuine openness and curiosity.

How to Perform

  1. Center yourself and form a clear question or intention
  2. Shuffle the deck while holding your focus
  3. Draw one card from the top, middle, or wherever feels right
  4. Sit with the card's imagery and meaning before reaching for a guidebook

Position Meaning

  • 1 The essence of your question or the energy/message most relevant to you right now

Interpretation Tips

With a single card, depth comes from exploring every facet: the upright meaning, what it would mean reversed, how the imagery speaks to you personally, what element and number are involved, and how it relates to your current life circumstances. A daily one-card practice is the fastest way to build intuitive fluency with the deck.

Three-Card Spread

Best For: Quick insights, storytelling, understanding flow

The three-card spread is the workhorse of tarot reading. Versatile enough for almost any question, it creates a narrative arc that gives context and direction. The three positions can be assigned many different meanings depending on your question.

Common Three-Card Frameworks

  • Past / Present / Future - Understand how past events led to the present and what is developing
  • Situation / Action / Outcome - Clarify where you are, what to do, and likely results
  • Mind / Body / Spirit - Check in with all dimensions of your wellbeing
  • You / Partner / Relationship - Explore relationship dynamics
  • Option A / Current Path / Option B - Compare choices

How to Perform

  1. Decide which three-card framework fits your question
  2. Shuffle while focusing on your question
  3. Draw three cards and lay them left to right
  4. Read each card individually, then look at the story they tell together

Positions (Past / Present / Future)

  • 1 Past: Events, energies, or influences from the past that are affecting the current situation
  • 2 Present: The current state of affairs, the energy surrounding you right now
  • 3 Future: The likely outcome or developing energy based on the current trajectory

Relationship Spread

Best For: Understanding relationship dynamics, compatibility, shared challenges

This five-card spread illuminates the dynamics between two people, whether romantic partners, friends, family members, or business associates. It reveals each person's energy, the nature of their connection, the challenges they face together, and the potential outcome of the relationship.

How to Perform

  1. Hold both people in your awareness as you shuffle
  2. Draw five cards and lay them in the pattern below
  3. Read positions 1 and 2 as individual energies, then 3-5 as shared dynamics

Positions

  • 1 You: Your current energy, feelings, and role in the relationship
  • 2 Partner: Their current energy, feelings, and role in the relationship
  • 3 Connection: The nature of what bonds you together, the heart of the relationship
  • 4 Challenge: The main obstacle or growth area for the relationship
  • 5 Outcome: Where the relationship is heading based on current energies

Celtic Cross

Best For: Complex situations, comprehensive life overviews, deep exploration

The Celtic Cross is the most famous and comprehensive tarot spread, using ten cards to provide a thorough analysis of any situation. It examines your present circumstances, challenges, past and future influences, conscious and unconscious factors, and the ultimate outcome. While complex, mastering this spread gives you a powerful tool for deep insight.

How to Perform

  1. Take extra time centering and forming your question clearly
  2. Shuffle thoroughly, as ten cards require good mixing
  3. Lay cards 1-2 as a cross in the center, cards 3-6 around them, and cards 7-10 in a column to the right
  4. Read the center cross first (situation and challenge), then the surrounding cards (influences), then the column (outcome path)

Positions

  • 1 Present: The heart of the matter, your current situation
  • 2 Challenge: The immediate challenge or crossing energy (laid horizontally over card 1)
  • 3 Past: Recent past events that created the current situation
  • 4 Future: Energy approaching in the near future
  • 5 Above (Conscious): Your conscious goals, what you are actively working toward
  • 6 Below (Unconscious): Subconscious influences, hidden factors, deeper motivations
  • 7 Advice: The recommended approach or attitude
  • 8 External: Environmental factors, how others see the situation
  • 9 Hopes/Fears: Your deepest hopes or fears about the outcome
  • 10 Outcome: The most likely outcome based on all present energies

Reading the Celtic Cross

Start with cards 1 and 2 to understand the core dynamic. Then read cards 3 and 4 for the timeline (what led here, what is coming). Cards 5 and 6 reveal the gap between what you know consciously and what operates beneath the surface. The column (7-10) tells the story of how the situation resolves.

Pay special attention when Major Arcana cards appear, as they indicate powerful archetypal forces at work. Notice the dominant suit for clues about whether the situation is primarily emotional (Cups), material (Pentacles), mental (Swords), or passion-driven (Wands).

Tips for All Spreads

  • Read connections: Look at how cards relate to each other. Do they tell a consistent story or reveal tension?
  • Notice patterns: Multiple cards from the same suit suggest that element's domain is central. Multiple Major Arcana indicate significant spiritual forces.
  • Trust the process: If a card seems irrelevant at first, sit with it. Its meaning often becomes clear with time.
  • Journal everything: Record your spreads and return to them later. You will be amazed at how accurate cards can be in retrospect.
