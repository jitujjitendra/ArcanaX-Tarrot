The 22 Archetypes

The Fool's Journey

The Major Arcana tells the story of a soul's evolution from innocence to enlightenment. Each of the 22 cards represents a stage in this universal journey of growth, challenge, and transformation.

Understanding the Major Arcana

The 22 Major Arcana cards are the heart and soul of the tarot deck. While the Minor Arcana deals with daily events and situations, the Major Arcana addresses the great themes of human existence: destiny, spiritual awakening, karmic lessons, and the archetypal forces that shape our lives. When these cards appear in a reading, pay special attention, as they signify moments of profound significance and soul-level growth.

The Fool's Journey is a narrative framework that connects all 22 cards into a single coherent story. It follows The Fool, the protagonist (and a stand-in for each of us), as they travel through life encountering teachers, challenges, transformations, and ultimate integration. This is not a linear path with a single ending, but a spiral that we travel repeatedly throughout our lives, gaining deeper understanding with each cycle.

The Journey Begins: Innocence and Discovery (Cards 0-7)

0 - The Fool

Element: Air | Keywords: Beginnings, innocence, spontaneity, faith

The journey begins with a leap of faith. The Fool stands at the edge of a cliff, carrying nothing but a small pack and infinite possibility. This card represents the moment before any journey begins, when we are pure potential unburdened by experience. It teaches us that every great adventure requires the courage to step into the unknown.

Working with Major Arcana in Readings

When Major Arcana cards appear in your readings, give them special weight. They indicate that powerful archetypal forces are at play in your life, and the situation involves soul-level growth rather than merely everyday circumstances. A reading dominated by Major Arcana suggests you are at a significant crossroads or going through a period of profound transformation.

Consider where each card falls in the Fool's Journey to understand the evolutionary stage it represents. Cards early in the sequence (The Magician, The Empress) relate to initial development and establishing foundations. Middle cards (Strength, The Hanged Man) involve deepening and testing. Later cards (The Star, The World) point toward integration and spiritual maturity.
