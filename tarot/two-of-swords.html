Two of Swords

💨 Air

The suspended moment between two truths where the blindfold of indecision becomes heavier than either blade.

▲ Upright Meaning

Blindfolded between two truths, you refuse to choose. But not choosing is itself a choice. Remove the blindfold - the discomfort of seeing clearly is less than the pain of prolonged denial.

▼ Reversed Meaning

Information surfaces that breaks the stalemate. Forced decision after prolonged avoidance. The blindfold falls whether you are ready or not.

Keywords

indecision stalemate avoidance difficult choice denial

Element: Air

Intellect, communication, truth, and clarity
